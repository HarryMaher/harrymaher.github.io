Below are examples of some of the data analysis that I have done over the past year. Please click on the images on the left to open the visualizations in a new window. Please email me with any questions.
Here is a set of interactive set of maps intended to allow users to explore how lifestyles influence (or don't influence) health outcomes at the Brazilian state level. It forms a part of my GIS final project.




This is a motion chart I created at DASIL using data from the FBI's Uniform Crime Reports and the US census. I analyzed, downloaded, and organized this data to create this vizualization.

Here is a map of camera traps at Iracambi, where I interned for one semester and created multiple new trail maps and collaborated with others to create a couple of maps planning future wildlife corredors. This map helps show the amount of wildlife diversity surrounding Serra do Brigadeiro.





This is an example of original research that I performed as an undergraduate at Grinnell College in a seminar-level class to see if we could reduce junk food consumption with social norms. In this experiment my lab partner and I either gave participants a fabricated "low-norm" condition or no such condition and observed the effect on junk food consumption.
